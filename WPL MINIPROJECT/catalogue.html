Waste Guide

Waste Segregation Catalogue

Tip: try keywords like plastic, food, battery, glass, paper, or chemical.

Wet Waste

Includes food leftovers, fruit peels, tea waste, and biodegradable kitchen waste.

Disposal tip: Put it in a wet waste bin and compost when possible.

Dry Waste

Includes paper, cardboard, clean packaging, and other non-biodegradable dry items.

Disposal tip: Keep it dry and send recyclable items for collection.

E-Waste

Includes chargers, cables, small electronics, batteries, and damaged gadgets.

Disposal tip: Never mix it with regular waste. Use authorized e-waste collection points.

Plastic Waste

Includes bottles, wrappers, containers, and single-use plastic materials.

Disposal tip: Rinse recyclable plastic before placing it in the dry waste stream.

Hazardous Waste

Includes chemicals, cleaning agents, paint containers, and expired medicines.

Disposal tip: Handle separately and do not dispose of it with household waste.

Metal and Glass Waste

Includes cans, glass jars, bottles, and small household metal scraps.

Disposal tip: Clean and separate safely to avoid injury and improve recycling quality.
